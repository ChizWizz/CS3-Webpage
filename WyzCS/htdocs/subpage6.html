Why Do We Share Our Feelings?

When we feel upset or troubled, we often talk about it with someone else. But why do we do this?

According to Jill Suttie, an expert on emotions in a 2021 article, our feelings serve as alarms, telling us when something's wrong, like feeling scared or angry. Even though these feelings are personal, we often tell others about them.

Suttie discusses Ethan Kross, a researcher who says we share our feelings to feel close to others. It's nice to know someone cares and listens when we're upset.

Talking about our feelings also helps us understand them better. When we say what's bothering us, it sometimes makes things clearer. It can even give us new ideas on how to handle the problem. But Kross warns that just talking without understanding our feelings might keep us upset.

So, sharing feelings isn't just about getting things off our chest. It's about feeling close to others and understanding ourselves better. It's like finding comfort and learning at the same time.

In a world where connections matter, talking about feelings helps us see how emotions work. It shows how important friends are and how we can deal with our feelings in a better way.
